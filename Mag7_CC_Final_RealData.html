1. Executive Summary

This report backtests a monthly covered call (CC) overlay strategy on the Magnificent 7 stocks over 2021-2025 using real option market data from ThetaData (actual bid/ask prices, not model estimates).

Critical finding: Unlike Black-Scholes model estimates that showed all 7 stocks benefiting from CC overlays, real market data reveals that CC overlays DESTROYED value on 3 out of 7 stocks — specifically the explosive growth stocks (NVDA, GOOGL, TSLA). The assignment costs from strong rallies far exceeded premium income.

2. Methodology & Data Sources

Strategy Definition

Buy & Hold: Invest $100K per stock in Jan 2021, buy shares (full lots where possible), hold continuously through Dec 2025.

CC Overlay: Each month, sell 1-month OTM call options against the position. Shares are always held and never sold.

Assignment Accounting: When stock closes above strike at expiration, assignment cost = (close - strike) × tradeable shares.

Data Sources (Real Market Data)

  • Stock prices: Yahoo Finance (split-adjusted) — used for B&H returns and expiration price checks
  • Option premiums: ThetaData API v3 — real bid/ask prices from actual market data
  • Option Greeks: ThetaData EOD Greeks — real delta, IV, and other Greeks from market data
  • Premium calculation: Mid-price = (bid + ask) / 2 — represents realistic execution price

Split Handling

Stocks with splits required special handling. Before the split date, option strikes are at pre-split prices; after the split, the share count increases proportionally:

  • GOOGL: 20:1 split (Jul 2022) — CC starts Aug 2022 (58 shares → 1,160 shares = 11 contracts)
  • AMZN: 20:1 split (Jun 2022) — CC starts Jul 2022 (31 shares → 620 shares = 6 contracts)
  • NVDA: 4:1 (Jul 2021) + 10:1 (Jun 2024) — 1 contract → 4 → 76 contracts
  • TSLA: 3:1 split (Aug 2022) — 1 contract → 4 contracts

GOOGL and AMZN had ~42 months of CC data (post-split only). With $100K, you cannot buy 100 shares of a $3,000+ stock.

7. Key Insights from Real Data

1. CC Overlay is NOT Universal — It Depends on Growth Rate

The most important finding: CC overlays destroy value on explosive growth stocks. NVDA (+1,326% B&H) saw -$173K in net CC loss. GOOGL (+265%) lost -$15K. TSLA (+85%) lost -$29K. The mechanism is simple: when a stock doubles or triples, even a 20-delta call gets assigned, and the assignment cost (upside forfeited) dwarfs the premium collected.

2. CC Works Best in Bear/Sideways Markets

2022 was universally profitable for CC overlays: AAPL +$6,846, MSFT +$7,764, TSLA +$14,860. Zero assignments with elevated IV. In contrast, 2023-2024 bull markets caused heavy assignment losses for most stocks. CC is essentially a volatility-harvesting strategy that profits from sideways movement.

3. Real Premiums vs Model Estimates

Black-Scholes model estimates previously showed all 7 stocks with positive CC net. Real market data reveals a starkly different picture:

  • MSFT: Model estimated ~+12% CC net → Real: +16.9% (model was too conservative!)
  • NVDA: Model estimated ~+152% CC net → Real: -173.1% (model was catastrophically wrong)
  • GOOGL: Model estimated ~+11% CC net → Real: -15.5% (model missed assignment severity)

The discrepancy comes from: (1) real bid-ask spreads reduce premium income, (2) actual assignment timing differs from model, (3) real IV fluctuations aren't captured by fixed monthly estimates.

4. Practical Recommendations

DO sell covered calls on: MSFT (moderate growth, low IV → steady income), AMZN (recovered post-split), AAPL (stable blue chip), META (moderate growth with elevated IV periods).

DO NOT sell covered calls on: NVDA (too explosive), GOOGL (strong multi-year trend), TSLA (extreme volatility causes frequent assignment in up-months).

General rule: If you expect a stock to grow >30%/year, covered calls will likely destroy value. Use them on stocks you expect to grow 5-20%/year.

8. Disclaimer & Data Notes

Data source: All option premiums (bid, ask, mid), deltas, and implied volatilities are from ThetaData API v3 — real historical EOD market data. Stock prices from Yahoo Finance (split-adjusted).

Execution assumption: Premium = mid-price (average of bid and ask). In practice, you'd likely execute between mid and bid, resulting in slightly lower premiums.

Split handling: For stocks with splits (GOOGL, AMZN, NVDA, TSLA), CC trading only occurred when the position had ≥100 shares for at least 1 contract. Pre-split GOOGL and AMZN required >$100K for 100 shares, so CC started post-split.

META note: FB→META ticker change (Jun 2022) caused missing option chain data for some early months.

Past performance does not guarantee future results. Options trading involves substantial risk.
