2. Methodology & Key Concepts

Strategy Definition

Buy & Hold: Invest $100K per stock in Jan 2021, buy full lots (100 shares), hold continuously through Dec 2025.

CC Overlay: Each month, sell 1-month OTM call options against the full position. Shares are always held and never sold.

Assignment Accounting: When stock closes above strike at expiration, assignment cost = (close - strike) x shares. This represents upside forfeited.

Key Concepts

Delta: Measures option price sensitivity to $1 stock move. Also approximates probability of expiring ITM. Higher delta = closer to ATM = more premium but more assignments.

Implied Volatility (IV): Market's expectation of future price fluctuation. Higher IV = higher premiums = more "fuel" for CC strategy.

Black-Scholes Model: Used to price options and calculate the strike for a given target delta. Risk-free rate = 4% annualized.

7. IV-Delta Relationship & Regime Analysis

How IV Interacts with Delta Selection

Low (20-25%)AAPL, MSFT0.08-0.18Low IV = low premiums. Very OTM needed to avoid assignment costs exceeding small premiums
Medium (28-32%)GOOGL, AMZN0.10-0.40Depends on growth more than IV. GOOGL (high growth) needs low delta; AMZN (flat) tolerates high delta
High (39-50%)META, NVDA0.18Large premiums but explosive moves create large costs too. Moderate delta balances both
Very High (58%)TSLA0.45Highest IV generates enormous premiums. High delta works because 2025's moderate growth favored aggressive selling
Key insight: Higher IV does NOT automatically mean higher optimal delta. The optimal is determined by the interaction between IV (premium size) and the stock's return distribution (assignment costs). When IV overpays for actual volatility, CC strategies win.

8. Discussion & Key Insights

The Asymmetry Problem

Covered calls have a fundamental asymmetry: premium income is bounded (you can only collect so much per month), but assignment costs are unbounded (the stock can rally 20%, 40%, even 60% in a single month). This is why CC strategies always underperform B&H in strong bull markets but always outperform in bear/flat markets.

Stock-Specific Insights

NVDA - The Premium Machine: Generated $398K in premiums on $100K at optimal delta. Assignment costs of $245K still left a massive $153K net. Works because NVDA's price appreciation massively increased per-share premiums over time.
AMZN - The CC Ideal Candidate: Relatively flat stock (+40% over 5 years) with medium IV (32%). CC overlay added +46.3% at delta 0.40 - more than doubling the effective return. Best "CC yield" stock among Mag 7.
TSLA - High Risk, Moderate Reward: Highest IV (58%) means highest premiums ($327K), but $308K in assignment costs nearly wipe it out. Net +20% requires aggressive delta (0.45) with extremely high volatility in both directions.

Tactical Adjustments

  1. Increase delta during bearish/uncertain periods - Premium income is maximized with minimal assignment risk.
  2. Decrease delta or pause CC before earnings/catalysts - Major announcements can trigger 10%+ moves.
  3. Monitor IV relative to historical levels - When IV is elevated, CC premiums are exceptionally rich.
  4. Review quarterly - If a stock's growth trajectory changes, recalibrate delta accordingly.

10. Appendix: Data Sources & Limitations

Data Sources

  • 2025 stock prices and IV: ThetaData API (option_history_greeks_eod) - verified quarterly from Jan through Dec 2025
  • 2021-2024 stock prices and IV: Compiled from publicly available historical data and ThetaData archives
  • Option pricing: Black-Scholes model with risk-free rate = 4.0%
  • Strike rounding: Nearest standard option increment ($0.50 for stocks <$50, $2.50 for $50-$200, $5 for >$200)

Limitations

  • Simplified option pricing (Black-Scholes vs real market bid-ask)
  • No transaction costs (commissions, spreads, slippage)
  • Monthly expiration only (weeklies may yield more but require more management)
  • No early assignment modeling for American options
  • Fixed risk-free rate (4%) vs actual variation from ~0% to ~5%
  • 2025 annual returns calculated from verified Dec 31, 2024 → Dec 31, 2025 closing prices (source: Macroaxis/exchange data)
